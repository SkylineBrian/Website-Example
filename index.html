PhD Student in Geography · Spatial Networks · Accessibility · Human Mobility

My research sits at the intersection of spatial network science and human mobility. I study commuting networks, disaster disruption of mobility, and multilayer network methods to understand accessibility and the structure of cities.

Research Highlights

U.S. Commuting Network Backbone

Applying the disparity filter to tract-level flows to reveal the backbone of commuting across CBSAs, then aligning flows to road networks for socio-economic analysis.

Networks Backbone CTPP

Childcare Accessibility (MCB2SFCA)

A modified commuter-based two-step floating catchment model to quantify childcare access in Hillsborough County, FL, comparing z-scores and visualizing spatial disparities.

Accessibility GIS

Multilayer Spatial Networks — Systematic Review

A PRISMA-guided review synthesizing how multilayer methods are used in social-spatial research, including benefits, spatial constraints, and risk-of-bias assessment.

Review PRISMA

Emergency Routing & Bridges (NetLogo)

Agent-based experiments in Berlin, NH to test how adding/removing bridges affects emergency travel times under heavy traffic conditions.

ABM Transport
